What is an IoT-Based Water Management System?

The IoT-Based Water Management System uses controller, sensors, meters, and other smart devices that are connected through web-based application, analytics tools. and such that can be viewed through mobile, and computer's dashboard. This project created from Arduino and ESP will be helpful when it comes to monitoring the retention level of water and its reusability when pouring it to plants to thrive.

Materials Used

In the makings of the IoT Water Management System, this will be the required materials to build the device.

  • ESP32 Development Board w Type-C Cable
  • Water Lever Sensor Analog
  • Ultrasonic Module - HC-SR04T
  • LEDs (5mm)
  • 2.5-6V 80-120L/H Micro Brushless Magnetic Driving Pump
  • Jumper Wires
  • Printed Circuit Board
  • Resistors - 750 Ohms

From this aforementioned materials, the group will be using only 2 different sensors to read the depth of the retained water as well as its level from the water that has been harvested by the storage.

Circuit Diagram For ESP-Based Water Management System

In the image above, this is the researchers' circuit diagram for Water Management System Using ESP32. This diagram can help the individual electronic components found on our activity to turn into a functional Water Management System. As one may see, we are using a few variety of sensors for measuring the parameters of water, this includes:

HC-SR04T Sensor - Uses mainly for sound waves. If the time measure for the pulse to travel is short, "The tank is on high load", and if the time it takes for the pulse to go back is long, "The tank is low"

Water Level Sensor (Analog) - uses for detecting water levels or presence

Driving Pump - to simulate the flow of water back.

Hardware Pinout of IoT Water Management System

HC-SR04T Sensor and ESP32

VCC 5v or 3.3v
TRIG GPIO 23
ECHO GPIO 22
GND GND

Water Level Sensor and ESP32

VCC 5v
SIG Analog Pin (An)
GND GND

Driving Pump

CNTRL GPIO 18 (MOSFET)
Power External
